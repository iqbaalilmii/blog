## About Me

Security Engineer specializing in **Digital Forensics & Incident Response (DFIR)**

Hi! I'm Iqbal, a high school student from Indonesia who is passionate about cybersecurity, especially Capture The Flag (CTF) competitions. I enjoy solving challenges across different categories, breaking things to understand how they work, and continuously learning new concepts through hands-on practice.

This blog is my personal learning journal. Here, I document CTF writeups, techniques, tools, and concepts that I encounter throughout my cybersecurity journey. One of the biggest reasons I write these posts is to reinforce my own understanding. I've found that solving a challenge once isn't enough—explaining it in my own words forces me to truly understand why a solution works, not just how to reproduce it. If these writeups help someone else along the way, that's an awesome bonus. I also enjoy sharing things I learn outside of competitions whenever I come across interesting topics worth documenting.


> The writeups on this blog document my analysis of attack patterns, forensic techniques, and both forensic and security research.
